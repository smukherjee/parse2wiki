**What makes this section succeed:** Named clients. Quantified results. Explicit mapping between past work and current RFP requirements. References who are prepared and willing to speak positively.

**What makes this section fail:** Anonymous case studies ("a large government agency"). Results without numbers. Case studies selected for impressiveness rather than relevance. Unprepared references. Stale case studies (older than the RFP's recency requirement).

**Selection criteria for case studies:**
1. **Relevance first** — choose the engagement most similar to this RFP's scope, even if it is not your most impressive project
2. **Recency second** — within the RFP's recency window (typically 3-5 years)
3. **Quantified results third** — engagements with measurable outcomes beat those with subjective praise
4. **Reference quality fourth** — a prepared, enthusiastic reference multiplies the case study's impact
